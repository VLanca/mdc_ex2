Krueger, artista pioneiro em Responsive Enviraments, procurou desenvolver uma pesquisa que consistia na exploração de novos meios, no qual a participação do público é ativa. Deste modo, os ambientes responsivos utilizavam o uso da tecnologia sensorial e computacional que reconheciam os movimentos humanos.
Computer Lib/Dream Machines, de Ted Nelson, um livro com duas capas. Em Computer Lib é abordado o funcionamento interno do computador para leigos, e, Dream Machines, o potencial dos computadores.
O conjunto dos dois textos que abordam a máquina e a ligação entre o Homem, ou seja, pretende-se que os sistemas e as máquinas possam passar a ser para qualquer pessoa, e que sejam interativos.


  • METAPLAY (1970), derivou da GLOWFLOW. Esta instalação enfatizou a interação entre o público e o ambiente, existindo uma interação em tempo real entre o artista e o público.

    “The image communications (Figure 25.2) started with na analogue data tablet which enabled the artist to draw or write on the computer screen. The person doing the drawing did not have to be an artist, but the term is used for convenience.” [3]

  • ENVIRONMENT AND PARTICIPANTS - “In order to focus on the relationships between the environment and the participants, rather than among participants, only a small number of people should be involved at a time.”[3]
    DYNAMIC PARTICIPATION - “Since the goal was to encourage the participants to express themselves through the environment, the program automatically responded to the footsteps of people entering the room with electronic sound.”[3]
    UNUSUAL DIALOGUE - “These facilities provided a rich repertoire for an unusual dialogue. The artist could draw pictures on the participants.”[3]
  • USER INTERFACE - “It will enter every home and office and intercede between us and much of the information and experience we receive.”[3]
    HARDWARE AND SOFTWARE - "The control system includes hardware and software control of all inputs and outputs as well as processing for decisions that are programmed by the artist. He must balance his desire for interesting relationships against the commitment to respond in real-time.”[3]

  • PSYCHIC SPACE (1971) - Ambiente de expressão musical e visual em que o objetivo era que o público interagisse no ambiente no qual estavam inseridos, existindo um programa que mediava a interação entre o humano e o sistema.

    “The participant’s position on the floor was the basis for each of the interactions. The sensing was done by a 16 2. 242 grid of pressure switches, constructed in 22 . 42 modules, each containing eight switches (Figure 25.5). Since they were electronically independent, the system was able to discriminate among individuals if several were present.”[3]

  • VIDEOPLACE (1975) - O vídeo era o principal foco desta interação, permitia uma experiência visual entre as pessoas que se encontravam nas salas.

    “VIDEOPLACE is a conceptual environment with no physical existence. It unites people in separate locations in a common visual experience, allowing them to interact in unexpected ways through the video medium. The term VIDEOPLACE is based on the premise that the act of communication creates a place that consists of all the information that the participants share at that moment.”[3]

  • HYPERTEXT -“Hypertext” means forms of writing which branch or perform on request; they are best presented on computer display screens.”[4]
    RESPONSIVE ENVIRONMENT -“This concept extends itself naturally to fantic space, the space and relationships sensed by a viewer of any medium, or a user in any presenting or responding environment.”[4]
    DIALOGUE -“Moreover, dialogue by simple systems tends to have a fake quality. It is by no means obvious that phony dialogue with a machine will please the student.”[4]

  • DIAGRAMA DE HIPERTEXTOS- texto que se agrega a outros blocos de textos por meio de referências específicas — hiperligações.

    “The computer display screen, however, permits footnotes on footnotes on footnotes, and pathways of any structure the author wants to create. Discrete, or chunk style, hypertexts consist of separate pieces of text connected by links.”[4]

  • PROJECT XANADU, The Nelson Organization’s - Biblioteca de Hipertexto, um editor de documentos que poderia ter substituído a Worl Wide Web.

    “Although we have not yet reached Xanadu, when one of these systems, the World Wide Web, began to explode in popularity during the 1990s(…)Xanadu was developed, indeed, originally for the purpose of implementing some of these functions, but the two split apart. It turned out that the Parallel Textface required an extremely unusual data structure and program techniques; these then became the Xanadu system.”[4]
